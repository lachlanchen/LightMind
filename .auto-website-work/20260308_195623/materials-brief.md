# Core Positioning
- LightMind is an HKU-origin deep-tech company (incorporated in Hong Kong on 2025-09-19) focused on low-power optical sensing and optical-computing modules.
- The public-facing product story should center on an always-on, first-person AI wearable and memory platform that converts optical/acoustic sensing into structured memory cues.
- Strategic positioning: "optical sensing + optical computing" hardware foundation, delivered through both consumer systems and partner-integrated modules.

# Product Lineup Facts
- Core module: low-power optical sensing and optical-computing hardware for edge perception, with partner integration interfaces and reference design/SDK direction.
- Consumer wearable system: all-day AI wearable in pendant/clip form factors; smart-glasses form factor is also referenced in 2026 seed materials.
- Software layer: companion mobile/cloud workflow that organizes semantic memory cues into a searchable memory graph (recall, recap, reminders, decision support).
- Business model is explicitly dual-track: B2C (device + subscription) and B2B (module sales, evaluation kits, integration/customization support).
- Stage of execution: principle module prototype and early wearable prototype/testing completed; next phases emphasize controlled pilots, small-batch refinement, and commercialization.

# Strongest Value Propositions
- Low-power, edge-first perception: optical front-end encoding reduces downstream compute, latency, bandwidth, and power burden.
- Memory utility over raw capture: system focuses on structured semantic cues for recall and assistance, not raw continuous video archive positioning.
- Always-on contextual assistant: supports timeline recall, daily recap, reminders, and decision support from first-person context.
- Defensible hardware moat: differentiation is rooted in proprietary optical sensing/computing modules, not only software UX.
- Scalable commercialization path: consumer product validates experience while B2B modules scale across robotics, industrial, autonomy, and edge-AI devices.

# Messaging Guardrails (claims to avoid or soften)
- Avoid absolute privacy/security claims (e.g., "fully private," "zero risk," "100% secure"); use "privacy-conscious" and "privacy-by-design" wording.
- Avoid over-claiming sensory scope (e.g., "full five-sense recording"); keep to optical/acoustic sensing and structured semantic memory cues.
- Avoid unverified hard claims from pitch context unless reconfirmed (e.g., "already on App Store," "existing orders," exact mass-production launch month).
- Avoid aggressive market-size/stat claims unless source-cited on-page (e.g., specific trillion-dollar TAM figures).
- Avoid implying current mass-market launch; present current stage as pilot/small-batch commercialization progression.
- Avoid regulated-domain performance claims (medical/automotive autonomy outcomes) unless explicitly validated and compliant.
- Avoid jargon-heavy hero language (e.g., long "multimodal platform" phrasing without user benefit).

## Hero
**Headline:** Always-on AI wearable for memory, recall, and better decisions.

**Subheadline:** LightMind combines low-power optical sensing and edge AI to turn daily experiences into searchable memory cues.

## Problem
People absorb too much information across too many devices, but critical context is still easy to lose. Traditional always-on capture approaches are power-hungry, noisy, and hard to trust for daily use.

## Solution
LightMind uses proprietary optical sensing and optical-computing modules to encode useful context at the edge. Instead of storing raw streams, the system builds structured memory cues and a memory graph for recap, recall, reminders, and decision support.
