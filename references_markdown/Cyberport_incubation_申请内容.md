2.3 Company Details:​
2.3.1 Objectives – the “why” of the company​
(Please state in clear and specific terms what the company aims to achieve)(*)​
LightMind exists to reshape how intelligent systems perceive the physical world by making multi-dimensional light-based
perception practical and deployable. Our objective is to develop and commercialize low-power, high-efficiency optical
sensing and optical-computing modules that capture and process richer optical information, including intensity, spectral,
polarization, phase and holographic cues, at the edge. By converting complex optical signals into usable representations
locally, we aim to reduce downstream compute load, latency and energy consumption, enabling always-on perception in
power- and size-constrained devices. We will provide compact, scalable hardware modules with clear integration interfaces
so partners can integrate advanced optical perception into real products efficiently. Through pilots and partnerships, we
aim to accelerate adoption in embodied AI, autonomous driving, industrial automation and consumer electronics, where
robust perception, high photon throughput and low power are critical. In parallel, we will build a consumer-facing
multimodal wearable as a flagship application to validate and showcase the platform’s value in all-day memory capture,
environment sensing and decision assistance, while continuously improving manufacturability, reliability and scalable sales
of our core modules.​

2.4 Implementation: (A summary of your company’s vision, mission and positioning.)​
2.4.1 Proposed Products / Services(*)​
We will deliver: (1) low-power optical sensing and optical-computing modules as standardized hardware building blocks
(module + interface + basic SDK/reference design) that enable multi-dimensional optical perception on edge devices; (2) a
consumer-facing multimodal always-on intelligent wearable powered by our module for continuous environment sensing
and all-day memory capture, enabling recall, context understanding, and decision assistance; and (3) integration support
and customization services for enterprise partners, including module integration, evaluation, and application-specific
tuning for embodied AI, autonomy, industrial sensing, or consumer electronics.​
2.4.2 Target Market / Clients and Market Potential (*)​
Our primary target clients for the core business are B2B customers who need robust perception under strict power/latency
constraints: embodied AI robotics developers, autonomous driving and ADAS suppliers, industrial automation and machine-
vision integrators, and consumer electronics manufacturers seeking differentiated sensing capabilities. The market
potential is driven by the rapid growth of edge AI and perception-centric products where conventional RGB cameras and
compute-heavy pipelines struggle with power budgets, bandwidth, and reliability. Our platform approach allows multiple
verticals to share the same core optical module technology, creating scalability across robotics, automotive, industrial
inspection, and next-generation smart devices. In parallel, our consumer wearable targets privacy-sensitive, high-
information-density users who benefit from always-on environment sensing and memory assistance, serving both as a
revenue stream and as a high-visibility proof-of-value for the core module platform.​
2.4.3 Competition Analysis: Any other similar or competitive products / services in the market? If
yes, how do you differentiate your products / services from them and what are your competitive
edges? (*)​
Competitive alternatives include conventional camera-based perception modules (RGB/IR cameras + ISP + heavy AI
compute), specialized sensors (LiDAR/ToF/thermal), and emerging wearable “lifelogging” devices that primarily rely on
standard imaging and cloud-heavy processing. Our differentiation is that we focus on multi-dimensional optical perception
enabled by integrated optics and optical computing at the edge, aiming to extract richer information while keeping power,
bandwidth, and latency low. Our competitive edges are: (a) multi-modal optical sensing
(spectral/polarization/phase/holography) to access information not available to human vision or standard cameras; (b)
edge-first processing enabled by optical computing concepts to reduce downstream compute and enable always-on
deployment; (c) modular, partner-friendly productization (standardized module form factor + interfaces + reference design)
that lowers integration friction; and (d) a dual-track strategy: B2B module sales for scalable industry adoption, plus a
flagship consumer wearable that showcases real-world value and accelerates iteration.​
2.4.4 Revenue Model / Revenue Plan (*)​
We plan a hybrid B2B + B2C revenue model. For B2B, revenue comes from selling optical sensing/optical-computing
modules (per-unit hardware sales) and charging for evaluation kits/engineering services for integration and customization;
as adoption grows, we may add licensing/royalty models for module IP or long-term supply agreements with key partners.
For B2C, revenue comes from hardware sales of the intelligent wearable and an optional subscription for memory and
decision-assistance features (e.g., search, recall, summaries, reminders), designed to create recurring revenue and support
continuous software improvement. Our near-term plan prioritizes module commercialization and partner pilots to establish
technical credibility and production readiness, while using the wearable as a flagship application and channel for product
validation and brand building.​

3.3.1 Manpower Distribution(*)​
At the current stage, the company’s core R&D is primarily carried out by the founder team, who are PhD students and serve
as full-time technical contributors to the company’s key technology development. Manpower is therefore mainly allocated
to (i) optical sensing and optical-computing module R&D (hardware/optics, system integration, and edge processing), (ii)
wearable system engineering (firmware, embedded software, app and backend), and (iii) algorithm development and
testing (signal processing, perception models, evaluation). In parallel, limited manpower is allocated to business
development and partner engagement (pilot coordination, customer requirements, and documentation), as well as basic
operations and compliance (finance, administration, and company governance). As the incubation period progresses, we
plan to gradually expand manpower by recruiting full-time R&D engineers and interns to support prototyping, verification,
and productization.​
3.3.2 Equipment Distribution(*)​
Equipment expenditure is mainly allocated to (i) optical sensing and optical module prototyping and measurement
equipment (optical benches, lenses/filters/components, light sources, calibration targets, and test fixtures), (ii) electronics
prototyping and validation (PCB fabrication, development boards, debugging tools, power measurement, and reliability test
fixtures), and (iii) computing resources for algorithm development and evaluation (workstations, GPUs, storage, and
development tools). Equipment spending will be prioritized toward items that directly improve prototype iteration speed,
module characterization, and manufacturability verification.​
3.3.3 Other Direct Costs(*)​
Other direct costs primarily include (i) prototype materials and consumables (optical/electronic components, mechanical
parts, housings, adhesives, cables, connectors), (ii) fabrication and assembly costs (small-batch PCB manufacturing, module
assembly, mechanical fabrication/3D printing/CNC, packaging), (iii) testing and certification-related costs (reliability tests,
safety tests, and preparation for relevant compliance where applicable), (iv) software/cloud service expenses needed for
development and pilot operation (server hosting, storage, and development licenses), and (v) business development and
pilot expenses (travel, pilot site setup, logistics, and customer demonstration materials). These costs scale with pilot volume
and small-batch builds.​
3.3.4 Forecast Income(*)​

B2C revenue from the consumer wearable device (early adopter sales) with optional subscription services for advanced
features. During the incubation period, we expect revenue to start with paid pilots/evaluation kits and small-batch module
shipments, then grow through repeat orders and longer-term supply discussions as pilots convert into commercial
